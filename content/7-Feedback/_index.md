### General Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  

During the **First Cloud AI Journey** program, I found the internship environment to be flexible, comfortable, and suitable for students. The program mainly followed an online self-study format, combined with optional office registration days and events. This allowed me to manage my own learning schedule, follow the program content, and complete the internship tasks at my own pace.

I appreciated the flexibility of the program because students could study from home, rewatch lesson videos, read documentation, and practice based on their own progress. This environment helped me develop self-discipline, self-learning ability, and the habit of researching new technologies independently. However, because most activities were online, direct interaction with other interns and professionals in the industry was still not very effective.

**2. Support from Mentors / Admin Team**  

The company members and support team provided helpful guidance and answered students’ questions during the learning, practice, and project implementation process. Although the number of students in the program was very large, with more than 700 participants, students could still ask questions through online channels when they needed support.

However, due to the large number of participants, it was difficult for the support team to follow each student closely. Since most communication happened through messages, the connection between students and mentors sometimes did not feel very direct. I think the program could be improved by adding more small-group Q&A sessions or project progress review sessions so that students can receive clearer feedback and communicate more effectively.

**3. Relevance to My Major**  

The program content was suitable for students in Information Technology, especially those who are interested in Cloud Computing, Networking, DevOps, and system architecture. During the internship, I learned about many core AWS services such as **Amazon VPC, Amazon EC2, Amazon S3, Amazon CloudFront, IAM, Amazon RDS, Amazon DynamoDB, Amazon CloudWatch, AWS Lambda, Amazon API Gateway, Amazon EventBridge, AWS Step Functions, and Amazon Bedrock**.

These topics helped me strengthen the knowledge I had learned at university while also expanding my understanding of how cloud systems are deployed in practice. In particular, through the group project **AI AWS Architecture Reviewer**, I had the opportunity to understand how multiple AWS services can be combined to build a complete cloud-based application.

**4. Learning Opportunities and Skill Development**  

The program provided many learning opportunities, especially for students who want to learn about AWS and cloud computing. Learning through videos, documentation, and hands-on labs helped me gradually approach AWS services from basic to more advanced levels.

Besides technical knowledge, I also improved several soft skills such as self-learning, documentation reading, note-taking, teamwork, task division, and report writing. Participating in events also gave me the chance to listen to real-world experience shared by professionals working in the industry, which helped me better understand the working environment and career direction in the cloud field.

**5. Culture and Teamwork**  

Because the program had a large number of participants and most activities were held online, the connection among all students was still limited. Most communication happened through messages, so students did not have many opportunities to interact directly or get to know people outside their own groups.

However, working on the group project was still a positive experience. Through teamwork, I was able to connect with a smaller group of students, exchange ideas, divide responsibilities, and support each other during the project implementation process. This helped me better understand how to collaborate in a project and take responsibility for shared tasks.

**6. Policies and Benefits for Interns**  

I appreciated that the program provided a flexible learning environment for students. Students could study online, follow the learning content, and join suitable activities based on their own schedules. This was very suitable for students because each person may have different learning conditions and time availability.

In addition, being able to participate in events, workshops, and sharing sessions with industry professionals was a valuable experience. These activities helped students not only learn technical knowledge but also gain practical insights into career development.

### Other Questions

**What was I most satisfied with during the internship?**  

The thing I was most satisfied with was the flexible and comfortable internship environment. I could arrange my own schedule to learn AWS content, review materials when needed, and practice according to my own progress. In addition, working on the group project helped me apply the knowledge I had learned to a real system, which allowed me to better understand how AWS services are combined in practice.

**What do I think the company should improve for future interns?**  

In my opinion, the program should improve the connection between students and between students and mentors or professionals in the industry. Since the number of students was very large, communication mainly through messages was not always effective for building strong connections.

I suggest organizing more small-group activities, such as networking sessions, regular Q&A sessions, project progress reviews, or career sharing sessions by topic. These activities would help students ask questions more easily, communicate more directly, and feel more connected to the program.

**Would I recommend this internship to my friends? Why?**  

If my friends are interested in cloud computing, AWS, DevOps, system architecture, or building applications on cloud platforms, I would recommend that they consider joining this internship program. The program is suitable for students who are willing to self-study, actively search for documentation, and learn AWS services in a structured way.

However, I would also let them know that the program requires a high level of self-discipline because most of the learning process is online and self-paced. If participants are proactive in learning, asking questions, and joining program activities, this internship can be a good opportunity to develop knowledge and career direction in the cloud field.

### Suggestions and Expectations

* Organize more small-group networking sessions so that students can connect with each other more effectively.
* Provide regular Q&A sessions with mentors or industry professionals so that students can ask questions directly.
* Add more project progress review sessions so that student teams can receive clearer feedback during implementation.
* Divide students into smaller groups based on interests such as Cloud, DevOps, Security, AI, or Backend to improve support and communication.
* Provide more real-world sharing sessions about cloud-related jobs, learning paths, and skills needed for future careers.
* Continue maintaining a flexible and comfortable internship environment, as this is very suitable for students.

Overall, I was quite satisfied with my internship experience in the **First Cloud AI Journey** program. The program helped me learn more about AWS, improve my self-learning ability, work in a team, and implement a practical project. If there are similar opportunities in the future, I would still be interested in participating to continue learning and developing my skills in the cloud computing field.
